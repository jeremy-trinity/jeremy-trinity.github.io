April 9: the four things you do first

Before opening any Udemy course, before watching a single lecture, you do these four things in this exact order. This takes about two hours and saves you from making expensive mistakes on day one.

Step 1: Test your free LIRN access before paying for anything

Go to link.gale.com/apps/?userGroupName=lirn17237 after your UoPeople Moodle login is active. Search for these six courses by name: Scott Duffy AZ-104, Imran Afzal Complete Linux Training, Kalyan Reddy AKS with Azure DevOps and Terraform, Alan Rodrigues AZ-500, Mumshad Mannambeth Docker for Absolute Beginners, Mumshad Mannambeth Kubernetes for Absolute Beginners. If all six appear, do not subscribe to the Udemy Personal Plan. Your course cost is EUR 0. If any are missing, subscribe to the Personal Plan at EUR 10 per month. That is your fallback.

Step 2: Create your Azure free account

Go to azure.microsoft.com/free. You get in credits for 30 days and 12 months of free services after that. The always-free tier includes one B1s Linux VM running 24/7, App Service F1 tier, Azure SQL 250GB, Blob Storage 5GB, and the AKS management layer at no cost. This is your primary lab environment for all of Phase 1 and Phase 2. Set up a budget alert at EUR 20 per month immediately after creating the account so you are notified before spending anything real.

Step 3: Set up your GitHub repositories right now

Create three repositories on github.com/jeremy-trinity: learning-log (daily study notes, commit every single day), azure-project-01 (your first portfolio project, Terraform VNet and VM), and cloud-engineering (scratch pad for code experiments). Read the Conventional Commits specification at conventionalcommits.org and apply it from the very first commit. This is the contribution graph that IBM and Deloitte recruiters will see on your profile before they ever read your CV. Build it from day one, not from month three.

Step 4: Bookmark these five free resources you will use every month

John Savill YouTube: youtube.com/@NTFAQGuy: the best free AZ-104, AZ-305, and AZ-400 study crams on the internet. Watch my study cram the week before every certification exam booking.
