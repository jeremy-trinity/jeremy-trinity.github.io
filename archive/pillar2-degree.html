The decision

Why UoPeople, and why restart instead of claiming equivalences

Two prior IT qualifications exist. EQF Level 3 in IT Systems Installation and Operation from Aveiro Professional School: hardware, OS, networking, IT support. EQF Level 5 in Health Information Systems from Fernando Pessoa University: clinical information systems, HL7 standards, health data management, database design, IT project management in healthcare. A BSc in Computer Science at Fernando Pessoa was paused due to medical circumstances.

Why not claim equivalences from the paused BSc? Technologies, cloud platforms, and software practices evolved substantially between that enrolment and 2026. Claiming equivalences would mean presenting credentials built on outdated coursework to IBM and Deloitte who operate on current standards. Starting again ensures every qualification reflects what the industry looks like now. This is precision, not modesty, and IBM and Deloitte understand and respect this argument when it is framed correctly.

UoPeople was chosen for four specific reasons: WASC accreditation (same body as USC, UCLA, Stanford), scholarship that may reduce tuition to zero for high-GPA students, LIRN library access that may reduce the entire bootcamp course cost to EUR 0, and 100% online format meaning medical flare days do not cause missed classes or lost deadlines.

The two-milestone structure

BSc vs Associate of Science, understanding the two-degree structure

The correct degree structure: The UoPeople BSc Computer Science is a 120-credit, 20-term, 4-year program. At approximately Term 10 (halfway, 60 credits, ~20 courses completed), you become eligible to request the Associate of Science in Computer Science, a formally recognized 2-year degree in its own right. This is not an automatic award; it must be explicitly requested from UoPeople once the 60-credit threshold and all Associate-level requirements are met. The Degree Handbook in these documents covers Terms 1-11 in detail because that period aligns with the 24-month Azure bootcamp. The BSc degree itself continues through Term 20.

Associate of Science (AS-CS)

~60 credits, ~20 courses, ~10 terms, ~2 years. Request this explicitly from UoPeople when the 60-credit threshold is reached. It is a formally recognized degree and adds an official US credential to the CV before the BSc is complete. Useful for job applications in Years 2-3 that ask for a degree qualification.

BSc Computer Science (BS-CS)

120 credits, ~40 courses, 20 terms, 4 years. The full degree. WASC-accredited. The target for Georgia Tech OMSCS admission. Terms 1-20. This document covers Terms 1-11 in bootcamp-aligned detail; Terms 12-20 continue with advanced CS coursework including theory, systems, security, and specialisation electives.

Why 11 terms are detailed here

Terms 1-11 are covered in depth because they run in parallel with the 24-month Azure Cloud Engineering bootcamp. After Term 11, the bootcamp is complete and the career plan enters active employment, but UoPeople continues alongside work at a sustainable pace through Terms 12-20, targeting BSc completion in Year 4.

Terms 12-20: continuing alongside work

After the bootcamp ends (Term 11), UoPeople continues at 1-2 courses per term. Advanced CS coursework: Theory of Computation, Computer Architecture, Advanced Algorithms, Software Engineering, Machine Learning, Capstone project. These terms are not detailed in this document because the bootcamp plan is the priority focus, they are studied alongside full employment at a target enterprise employer.

Money mechanics

Scholarship protection. GPA is a financial lever, not just an academic one

The UoPeople scholarship is GPA-dependent. Maintaining 3.7+ keeps it active. Losing it means paying tuition that scholarship students do not pay. The bootcamp always slows down when UoPeople grades are at risk. Never the other way around.

Write early, not on deadline

Every assignment: first draft three days before the deadline. Revise the day before. Submit with 24 hours spare. A medical flare on deadline day then costs nothing.

Discussion posts: depth over volume

Discussion posts are typically 20-30% of each grade. Three substantive posts that genuinely engage with the material beat ten superficial ones. Read what peers wrote. Respond to the actual argument.

Double-count study time

Write Python assignments as cloud-adjacent scripts wherever the topic allows. A loops exercise can parse Azure cost export CSV files. This double-counts study time and produces portfolio-adjacent work from academic work.

Read feedback as language practice

Every grade below 90% includes feedback. Read it specifically for language issues, not just content. At B2 English, the gap to 90%+ on written work is often register and precision, not understanding.

Know the UoPeople cycle

Terms are 8 weeks normal study, week 9 exams only, week 10 recovery and planning. Accelerate bootcamp in week 10. Slow bootcamp in week 9. Build this into the weekly calendar now.

Grades always beat bootcamp speed

Any week where UoPeople assignments are at risk: bootcamp pauses until grades are secured. The bootcamp can compress or expand. The scholarship, once lost, cannot be recovered.
