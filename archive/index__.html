Strategic context

Why this system, why now

This is not a learning experiment. It is a structured path from current position to employed cloud engineer, built around a medical reality, a geographic opportunity, and a precise career target. Every decision here has a reason.

The medical constraint is real

Spinal and pain conditions mean any role must be viable from a bed, from a remote setup, on unpredictable days. Cloud engineering is one of the few technical roles where a senior professional can operate entirely remotely, even from day one of an internship. DevOps was considered and deprioritised because it typically requires more office presence in early career stages. Cloud engineering, particularly Azure administration and infrastructure-as-code work, is the most work-from-home-compatible technical role available at the junior level.

Why Azure and not AWS

IBM Softinsa Viseu and Deloitte Portugal Technology and Transformation are the two immediate targets. Both operate primarily in Azure environments serving enterprise clients running Microsoft infrastructure. Cross-referencing live job postings in 2025 and 2026 confirms that 70% or more of cloud engineering roles in Portugal and France specify Azure as the primary platform. AWS is a Phase 4 addition, not a starting point. Building Azure depth first maximises the chance of getting an internship or junior role within 12 months.

Why Udemy as a second university

Udemy instructors operate as small businesses. The price point means they must sell volume to profit, which means the product must be good enough to receive few refund requests. Students can claim 100% money back within 30 days. This market mechanism creates a self-correcting quality filter. The best-selling Azure, Linux, Kubernetes, and Terraform courses have been reviewed by tens of thousands of students and survived. That is a more reliable quality signal than many traditional educational products at far higher prices.

Why 20% work experience from day one

Tech hiring is portfolio-driven. No amount of certifications or coursework replaces the question: have you worked in a real environment? Three afternoons per week of internship observation, even unpaid, produces questions that courses cannot generate. The format proposed is 3 afternoons per week, Monday, Wednesday, and Friday, leaving full mornings for deep bootcamp work and full days between each internship session for consolidation. This ratio of 20% real-world exposure to 80% structured study is calibrated for maximum learning velocity at minimum burnout risk.

The core philosophy: Be employable first, knowledgeable second. The bootcamp is the primary track. Everything else runs in parallel to support it, not compete with it. University grades matter because a strong GPA keeps the scholarship active. Languages matter because they expand the market. Reading matters because it builds the mental habits that make everything else possible. But if the bootcamp falls behind, everything else slows down until it is back on track.

Pillar 2

BSc Computer Science, University of the People

Academic foundation. Scholarship maintenance. Harvard CS50x in parallel.

🎓

UoPeople provides the academic credential that opens doors requiring a degree. The scholarship is GPA-dependent, which means consistent high grades are financially necessary, not just academically desirable. The degree also gives access to LIRN resources including Gale Presents Udemy, which potentially makes the entire Udemy bootcamp cost zero.

Harvard CS50x runs in parallel to Terms 1 through 3. It is treated as a companion to the bootcamp, not as a separate heavy commitment. Weeks 0 through 4 of CS50x (C, arrays, algorithms, memory) run alongside Term 1 UoPeople coursework and reinforce the foundations being built in both tracks simultaneously.

Daily time budget

2 to 3 Pomodoros per day for UoPeople written assignments, discussion posts, and readings. UoPeople takes the Monday and Thursday morning slots for written work, where written assignments are the priority before bootcamp deep work. Discussion posts are written in the evening Pomodoro.

Harvard CS50x and parallel programs

CS50x (cs50.harvard.edu/x) produces a free certificate on OCW by scoring 70% on all problem sets and the final project. CS50P (cs50.harvard.edu/python) runs alongside CS50x during lighter weeks and produces a second free Harvard certificate. CS50 SQL (cs50.harvard.edu/sql) is a Phase 3 addition. These three Harvard certificates cost nothing and carry the Harvard name, which is the strongest free academic credential available.

Scholarship protection strategy

The UoPeople scholarship is GPA-linked. Target GPA 3.7 or above across all terms. Write all assignments two days before the deadline. Use the discussion post to engage genuinely with peers, which typically accounts for 20% to 30% of grades. If a single week is particularly heavy in the bootcamp, the bootcamp slightly reduces pace before UoPeople grades are ever put at risk.

Pillar 3

Language Acquisition

English to C2, Spanish to B2, Mandarin to A2. Weekend study, daily micro-sessions.

🌍

Languages are a force multiplier, not a standalone goal. Each language unlocks a larger market, a richer professional network, and faster access to the best learning resources in that language. The principle is staggered acquisition: never study two languages at the same proficiency level at the same time, because interference slows both. Each language must be at a meaningfully different level from the others.

Why multilingualism matters for this career specifically

Being able to study Udemy courses in Portuguese, English, and French means access to three times the instructional content and three times the professional communities. A problem that has no English-language solution on Stack Overflow often has one in Portuguese or French. A certification study group on Discord that is too fast-moving in English might be exactly the right pace in Portuguese. This is a direct learning velocity advantage that most competitors do not have.

Pillar 5

Reading Program

One book per month. Technical, mindset, and career. Physical books, screens off.

📚

Reading is the one pillar that happens entirely offline. The 21:40 screens-off rule creates a natural reading window from 21:40 to 22:00 and again from 22:00 to 22:40 during the last meal. Physical books, read in bed if needed. One book per month is achievable at roughly 20 minutes per day. Two books per month is achievable on better weeks.

The selection logic: technical books are chosen for direct bootcamp application. Mindset and productivity books are chosen for system sustainability. Career and business books are chosen for the entrepreneurial context of the long-term plan.
