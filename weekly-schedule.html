Weekly Pomodoro Framework

A pillar-based allocation system built around a 40-minute work / 10-minute break cycle. Two modes adapt to any life period, constrained weeks with external commitments eating into study hours, and full weeks with maximum available depth. The distribution never changes; only the total volume shifts.

Note on the 70% rule: Bootcamp and extra courses always own the majority of study time. This is not negotiable. University work keeps its weekly obligation but does not expand into bootcamp territory. Languages and reading are maintenance, they ensure you do not lose progress, but they never compete with the core skill-building track.

Non-negotiables

Fixed daily anchors

🍽️
6 meal breaks daily
Medical requirement. They are fixed in the schedule and nothing competes with them. Approximate timing: morning, mid-morning, lunch, afternoon, evening, late evening.
⚙️
1 daily admin block
Every day, 7 days a week. GitHub commit or push. LinkedIn activity. Email triage. Tomorrow's plan. This is what makes the GitHub contribution graph green and the LinkedIn presence consistent.
📵
Screens off, 40 min before sleep
Non-negotiable for sleep quality. Set a daily alarm. Everything unfinished moves to tomorrow, sleep protects the study capacity of the next day.
🌿
Sunday recovery
Deep work and labs are prohibited on Sundays. Light university review and admin only. Recovery is not wasted time. It is what allows the next six days to be productive.
How to use this framework

Execution principles

01
Protect the morning
The first study block of the day always goes to bootcamp. No exceptions. Social media, email, and messages only open after the first pomodoro is complete.
02
Bootcamp is non-compressible
On constrained weeks the total volume drops, but bootcamp's share stays at 70%. University, languages, and reading compress, bootcamp does not.
03
Active beats passive
Labs, writing code, and solving problems count as full pomodoros. Watching a video without stopping counts as half a pomodoro at best. Build, break, fix, always.
04
Commit something daily
Every day ends with at least one GitHub commit. Even a README update or a commented-out Terraform block. The contribution graph is public and it tells a story before the CV is read.
05
Don't split bootcamp sessions
A single 40-minute bootcamp block is not enough for lab work. On days with 2+ available bootcamp pomodoros, run them consecutively before switching. Deep context takes time to build.
06
Weekly review on Saturday
End every Saturday with a 10-minute review: how many pomodoros per pillar this week? What was completed? What is next? This replaces the need to constantly update the schedule.
